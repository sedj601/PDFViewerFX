11.1.2. Capital Receipt
Capital receipt is one which is invested in the business for a long period. It
includes long term loans obtained from others and any amount realised on sale
of fixed assets. It is generally non-recurring in nature.
Characteristics
i. Amount is not received in the normal course of business.
ii. It is non-recurring in nature.
Examples
i. Capital introduced by the owner
ii. Borrowed loans
iii. Sale of fixed asset
11.2 Revenue Transactions
The business transactions, which provide benefits or supplies services
to a business concern for an accounting period only, are known as revenue
transactions. Revenue transactions can be Revenue Expenditure or Revenue
Receipt.
11.2.1 Revenue Expenditure
Revenue expenditures consist of those expenditures, which are incurred
in the normal course of business. They are incurred in order to maintain the
existing earning capacity of the business. It helps in the upkeep of fixed assets.
Generally it is recurring in nature.
Characteristics
i. It helps in maintaining the earning capacity of the business concern.
ii. It is recurring in nature.
